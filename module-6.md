Inputs

- Purchase price: The upfront cost of acquiring the machine or equipment.
- Installation cost: Expenses for setup, assembly, and commissioning in the factory.
- Energy consumption & energy price: Electricity usage of the machine and the corresponding cost per kWh.
- Maintenance & consumables: Cost of regular servicing, spare parts, and materials used during operation.
- Labor cost: Wages and related expenses for operators, technicians, and supervisors running or maintaining the machine.
- Machine lifetime: Expected operational lifespan (years) used to spread costs over time.

-------------------------------------------------

Calculations

- Cost per year: Annual cost of running the machine, including energy, labor, and maintenance.
- Cost per unit: Cost of producing one unit based on total operating expenses and production volume.
- Total lifetime cost (TCO): Complete cost of owning and operating the machine over its entire expected life.

-------------------------------------------------

Outputs

- Total TCO value: Final monetary value representing the full ownership cost.
- Cost breakdown: Detailed view of how much is spent on purchase, energy, maintenance, labor, etc.
- Comparison between machines/options: Helps choose the most cost-effective option among multiple machines or suppliers.
